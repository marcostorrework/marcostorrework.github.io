Description of the dashboard "Income and employment by major"

Data set

Information relevant to college majors for recent university graduates. Obtained from the American Community Survey 2010-2012 public use microdata series, with information on basic earnings and labor force information.

Source: FiveThirtyEight, data set "college-majors"

Dashboard

Level 1: Overview of categories

The chart provides an overview by major category.

For every major category, the chart shows:

  • Percent of employed graduates
  • Median income (USD) of graduates employed full-time (35+ hours) year-round
  • Global average: Average of median incomes (USD) across all majors (red line)
  • Number of students (hover the mouse on the blue bar to see this)
To drill down into a specific category, use the drop-down at the top. This takes to level 2.

Level 2: Details by major for selected major category

This page contains data by major. It is composed of two sections:

The top section shows, for every major in the category:

  • Percent of employed graduates, split into:
    • Percent of graduates employed in jobs requiring college
    • Percent of graduates employed in jobs not requiring college
  • Median income (USD) of graduates employed full-time (35+ hours) year-round
  • Average of median incomes (USD) across all majors (red line)
  • Global average (red line): Average of median incomes (USD) across all majors
  • Category average (grey line): Average of median incomes (USD) within the selected major category

The bottom section displays three pie charts with the following information for the selected major category:

  • Employment Type: Within employed graduates, percent working full-time (35+ hours) vs part-time
  • Gender: From the total of graduates, percent of men and women
  • Low Wage Employment: Within employed graduates, percent having low-wage service jobs
